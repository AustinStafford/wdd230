News

We all know where unowned animals such as dogs and cats end up most of the time, the pound. After a certain amount of time, if an animal is not adopted, it is put down, regardless of its health. Because of this, there are many animals' lives that are cut short. But what if this could change? What if a difference could be made in preventing unneccisary loss of animal life? Well, one business has made a difference! A local 17 year old boy, who has thought on this problem and possible solutions to it, founded "Paws for Life," a business dedicated to perserving the lives of animals through training programs with first responders. Instead of putting unadopted animals to sleep, they instead be inducted into programs where they too can make a difference in people's lives. Police departments could take K-9s and train them for K-9 units. Firefighter departments could adopt an animal to be its mascot. Other animals could be trained to be service animals. The possibilities are endless. But one thing is certain. Regarless of species, every life has meaning and purpose.

Events

Ready for a chance to network and make connections with other businesses?! Come join us for the annual RVA Commerce Cook Out!
