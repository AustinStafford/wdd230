Hero Image & Call to Action

We all know where unowned animals such as dogs and cats end up most of the time, the pound. After a certain amount of time, if an animal is not adopted, it is put down, regardless of its health. Because of this, there are many animals' lives that are cut short. But what if this could change? What if we could make a difference in preventing unneccisary loss of animal life? Well, we can make a difference! A local 17 year old boy recently propsed that instead of putting unadopted animals to sleep, they instead be inducted into programs where they too can make a difference in people's lives. Police departments could take K-9s and train them for K-9 units. Firefighter departments could adopt an animal to be its mascot. Other animals could be trained to be service animals. The possibilities are endless. But one thing is certain, regarless of species, one thing is certain. Every life has meaning and purpose.

News

RVA Commerce is proud to announce the brand new membership level of NP Membership, NP standing for non-profit. This new membership level is for non-profit organizations. This membership requires no fee. One of the benefits of this membership is that it helps non-profit organizations connect with other non-profit organizations over common goals. This benefits such organizations as it will help them to not only grow, but to build stronger foundations that will ensure their existance and the effect they have on the community. These connections will help non-profit organizations have greater influence in their common quest to help others.

Spotlight 3

U.S.A tech is a company built by former military recruites who have served in the military and have since retired. After their service, this band of brothers came together to build U.S.A. Tech, a company that makes and sells military approved tactical gear for both military and civilian use. "We want our military personal to be in good hands. We want those same hands to educate and train civilians in the case of self defense or to be prepared for an unforseen scenario." Ten percent of each purchase will go towards the Wounded Warrior Charity, a charity focused on the well fare of soldiers wounded in battle and to ensure these soldiers receive the help they need to fully recover and live normal lives.
